Desert Hills & Unincorporated Cave Creek

Desert Hills and the broader unincorporated Maricopa County areas north and west of Cave Creek proper are the value acreage alternative for buyers whose budget falls short of in-town corridor prices but whose requirements — horse-zoned desert acreage, Maricopa County, within reach of Phoenix — remain unchanged. The trade-off is infrastructure self-sufficiency over municipal services: private wells, septic, often private road access, and County zoning rather than the Town's Desert Rural ordinance.

What the Area Offers

The defining characteristic is more land per dollar. Parcels in Desert Hills tend to run larger than Stagecoach Pass or Mesquite Ranch at comparable or lower price points. Lower density means more privacy, more turnout room, and more distance between neighbors. The terrain is open Sonoran Desert — flat to gently rolling, with the same alluvial soil drainage characteristics that make desert arenas functional. For buyers running a multi-horse operation who need multiple paddocks, a round pen, an arena, hay storage, and equipment room, Desert Hills often delivers better value per acre than the in-town corridors.

Jurisdiction: Unincorporated Maricopa County

Most of Desert Hills is in unincorporated Maricopa County, not the Town of Cave Creek. The governing zoning is County code, not the Town's Desert Rural ordinance. Pull the exact County zoning designation for any specific parcel — confirm permitted uses, animal limits, and whether commercial boarding or training would require a Conditional Use Permit — from Maricopa County Planning and Development, not from the listing or assumptions. The Cave Creek mailing address does not imply Town jurisdiction.

Water, Wells and Access

Private wells and on-site septic are standard in Desert Hills. Confirm well permit status, depth, yield, and allowed uses with the Arizona Department of Water Resources, and test water quality for arsenic, bacteria, nitrates, and the full ADHS-recommended panel. Many access roads in the area are private — confirm that recorded maintenance agreements exist, and that the road accommodates horse trailers and emergency vehicles before closing.

Key Takeaways

  • Value acreage corridor — larger parcels and lower per-acre prices than in-town Cave Creek corridors.
  • Mostly unincorporated Maricopa County — County zoning governs, not the Town's Desert Rural ordinance.
  • Private wells and septic are standard — well and septic due diligence is the center of any purchase.
  • Private road access common — confirm recorded maintenance agreements and trailer access.
  • Confirm the exact County zoning designation and permitted uses with Maricopa County planning staff before writing.
