Cave Creek Parks & Recreation Directory

Cave Creek's outdoor recreation profile is one of the strongest of any small town in Maricopa County — and one of the primary reasons buyers move here from Phoenix, Scottsdale, and out of state. Within the 85331 zip code or immediately adjacent to it: two Maricopa County parks totaling more than 5,000 acres, one of the largest regional trail networks in the Phoenix metro, the 315-mile Maricopa Trail, a Town-operated desert nature park, a neighborhood community park, and three golf courses. This directory covers the verified public facilities with addresses, fees, hours, and honest notes on what each offers.

Maricopa County Parks

Cave Creek Regional Park

37019 N. Lava Lane, Cave Creek, AZ 85331  ·  (623) 465-0431  ·  maricopacountyparks.net
MARICOPA COUNTY · Entry fee

A 2,922-acre Maricopa County park dedicated in 1992, located 1.5 miles north of Carefree Highway on 32nd Street inside the Town of Cave Creek. Elevation ranges from 2,000 to 3,060 feet through classic upper Sonoran Desert — saguaro, brittlebush, palo verde, and the geological formations and mining remnants from Cave Creek's gold-rush era. The Go John Trail (6 miles) loops around the central mountain and is one of the most popular trails in the North Phoenix area. Ranger-led programs run regularly; check the county website for current schedules.

Facilities: Campground with large private sites; 51 individual day-use picnic sites; nature center and visitor center; gift shop; playgrounds; dog-friendly (leashed); restrooms. Horse trailer parking with hitch rails and water available. ADA: some facilities accessible.

Trails: 15+ miles of multi-use trails for hiking, mountain biking, and equestrian use. Trail distances range 0.2 to 5.8 miles, easy to challenging. The Slate Trail (shorter, easier) and Go John Trail (6-mile loop) are the most-traveled. The 315-mile Maricopa Trail passes through the park.

Camping: Available; $32/night plus $8 non-refundable reservation fee. Reserve through Maricopa County Parks or Recreation.gov.

Hours: Sun–Thu 6 a.m.–8 p.m.  ·  Fri–Sat 6 a.m.–10 p.m.  ·  Gates close nightly.  Entry fee: ~$7/vehicle. Hike/bike/equestrian $2/person. Maricopa County Annual Pass ($85/person) covers all county parks.

Spur Cross Ranch Conservation Area

44000 N. Spur Cross Road, Cave Creek, AZ 85331  ·  (602) 506-2930  ·  maricopacountyparks.net
MARICOPA COUNTY · $3/person

The only designated conservation area in the Maricopa County Parks system — 2,154 acres of upper Sonoran Desert 5 miles north of Cave Creek, designated on January 9, 2001, and celebrating its 25th anniversary in January 2026. The park has shared ownership between the Town of Cave Creek, the State of Arizona, and Maricopa County; Maricopa County Parks operates and maintains it. Nearly 90 documented archaeological sites reflect Hohokam presence from approximately A.D. 500–1250. Petroglyphs remain visible throughout the landscape.

What makes it different: Spur Cross is the only park in the Maricopa County system with a riparian area — Cave Creek (the waterway) runs seasonally through the park, typically November through March and after heavy rains. Cottonwood, willow, and mesquite line the creek bottom, creating a markedly different habitat from the surrounding desert. Over 80 bird species have been documented here. One of the densest stands of saguaro cactus in the region.

Trails: Eight trails, approximately 10 miles total. Easy to challenging. Popular routes include the Dragonfly Loop (1.8–2.6 miles, family-friendly, riparian access), the Jewel of the Creek Trail (riparian zone, cottonwoods), and the Elephant Mountain Loop (7.3 miles, significant elevation gain). The 315-mile Maricopa Trail passes through. Dogs welcome on leash.

Hours: May 1–Oct 31: 5 a.m.–9 p.m.  ·  Nov 1–Apr 30: 6 a.m.–7 p.m.  Entry fee: $3/person ages 15+, cash only. Cave Creek residents are eligible for an annual pass for $1/year (see Resident Perks below).

Town of Cave Creek Parks

Gateway Desert Awareness Park

38100 N. Vermeesch Road, Cave Creek, AZ 85331  ·  (480) 488-1400  ·  azfcf.org
TOWN OF CAVE CREEK · Free

The Town of Cave Creek's 26-acre community park — a Sonoran Desert nature park rather than a conventional grass-and-swings setup. Features children's play structures, an informative nature trail through native desert landscaping, an amphitheater, a demonstration garden identifying local plant species, and restrooms. Managed in partnership with the Arizona Foundation for Communities and Families. Ideal for families with young children learning desert ecology. Free admission.

Desert Willow Park

30832 Desert Willow Parkway West, Cave Creek, AZ 85331  ·  (approx. 4040 E Desert Willow Pkwy W)
FREE · 5:30 a.m.–10 p.m.

The primary neighborhood community park serving the Tatum Ranch area of Cave Creek — the go-to green space for South Cave Creek residents. A conventional community park with grass fields, trees, and full recreational amenities, adjacent to the Desert Willow residential collection in Tatum Ranch.

Facilities: Basketball court; sand volleyball court; soccer/multi-use fields; playgrounds (children of all ages); covered picnic ramadas with BBQ grills; walking/biking path loop; restrooms. Free admission. Open 5:30 a.m.–10 p.m. daily. Dog-friendly (leashed).

Dove Valley Park

E. Rancho Paloma Drive, Cave Creek, AZ 85331
FREE

A neighborhood park serving the Dove Valley Ranch and surrounding area of Cave Creek. Provides passive park space, green area, and walking access in the Dove Valley corridor. Coordinates the open space character that Dove Valley Ranch promotes as a community value alongside its golf course and desert setting.

Trails & Open Space

Cave Creek Town Trail Network

The Town of Cave Creek maintains a network of multi-use trails connecting neighborhoods to the county parks and open space. Trails link to Cave Creek Regional Park, Spur Cross Ranch Conservation Area, the Tonto National Forest, and the 314-mile Maricopa Trail. Uses permitted vary by trail segment — hiking, biking, and equestrian uses are distributed across the network. Trail maps are available at the Cave Creek Tourism Bureau at 6061 E. Cave Creek Road (immediately west of the Dairy Queen), (480) 525-2049.

State trust land note: State trust land borders many of the county parks and open spaces within the Town. A permit must be secured from the Arizona State Land Department for recreational use of state land. Do not assume that open desert adjacent to a park is freely accessible — verify land status before riding or hiking off designated trails.

The Maricopa Trail

A 315-mile non-motorized trail that circumnavigates Maricopa County, linking 10 regional parks. The trail passes through both Cave Creek Regional Park and Spur Cross Ranch Conservation Area, making Cave Creek a practical staging point for longer route segments. The Maricopa Trail is open to hiking, mountain biking, and equestrian use throughout the Cave Creek sections. Trail maps and current conditions at maricopacountyparks.net.

Resident Perks — What Cave Creek Homeowners Get

Owning a home within the Town of Cave Creek limits comes with a notable benefit: discounted access to Spur Cross Ranch Conservation Area. Town residents are eligible for an annual pass to Spur Cross for $1 per year (versus the $3/person daily fee for non-residents). Passes can be purchased in 1, 5, or 10-year increments at Cave Creek Town Hall, 37622 N. Cave Creek Road, Monday–Thursday 8 a.m.–4 p.m. A photo is required at time of issue. Note: this benefit applies to residents within the Town of Cave Creek limits — not all 85331 addresses qualify. Confirm your parcel's jurisdiction before assuming eligibility.

Maricopa County Annual Passes ($85/person) cover day-use access to all Maricopa County regional parks — including both Cave Creek Regional Park and Spur Cross Ranch — across more than 120,000 acres of parkland. Available at any county park entrance or online at maricopacountyparks.net.
