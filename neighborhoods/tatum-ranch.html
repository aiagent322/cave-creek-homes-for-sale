Tatum Ranch & South Cave Creek

Tatum Ranch and the broader South Cave Creek area form the entry tier inside the 85331 zip — a master-planned community of roughly 3,400-plus homes across many sub-neighborhoods that shares the Cave Creek mailing address while operating somewhat separately from old-town Cave Creek in character and buyer pool. For buyers who want the Cave Creek lifestyle and trail-and-town access at a far lower price point, this is the affordable on-ramp.

It is important to be clear about what this corridor is and is not. Most lots in the master-planned area are smaller and are not horse-zoned; this is primarily a value-and-amenity tier, not a horse-acreage corridor. The full Cave Creek experience — the Old West town core, Spur Cross trails, the Tonto National Forest — is a short drive away and accessible from a Tatum Ranch address, but horse-keeping on the lot itself depends entirely on the specific parcel's zoning.

Character and Amenities

South Cave Creek and Tatum Ranch offer the best dollar-per-amenity ratio inside the 85331 zip: master-planned neighborhoods, the Cave Creek Unified School District, golf, and a mix of gated and non-gated pockets. Median pricing here runs well below the old-town acreage tiers, which is exactly why it draws first-time buyers, snowbirds, and value-seeking remote workers discovering the area.

Horse-Keeping: Verify Before You Assume

Do not assume a Cave Creek mailing address means horse privileges. The 85331 zip spans the Town of Cave Creek, Carefree, Phoenix, Scottsdale, and unincorporated Maricopa County, so the zoning map matters far more than the mailing address. If keeping horses on your own lot is essential, this is usually not the corridor — look to Stagecoach Pass, Mesquite Ranch, Tonto Hills, or Desert Hills instead. If you want the lifestyle and trail access with boarding nearby, Tatum Ranch can be an excellent value entry point.

Typical Properties and Price Range

The area spans villas and smaller-lot single-family homes starting in the $400,000s up through larger homes, with the entry tier offering the broadest financing accessibility in the Cave Creek market. Confirm current comparable sales on ARMLS, and if horse use matters, verify the specific lot's zoning and any HOA animal rules before writing.

Key Takeaways

  • The affordable on-ramp to the Cave Creek lifestyle — master-planned, amenity-rich, from the $400Ks.
  • Shares the Cave Creek mailing address but operates separately from old-town acreage in character.
  • Most lots are not horse-zoned — verify zoning on any specific parcel before assuming horse privileges.
  • Best fit for first-time buyers, snowbirds, and value seekers who want trail-and-town access nearby.
  • Cave Creek Unified School District and golf are part of the draw at this price point.
