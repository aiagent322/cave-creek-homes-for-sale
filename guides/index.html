Cave Creek Homes For Sale Buyer's Guides

Buying residential property in Cave Creek requires due diligence that a standard residential purchase does not. These five guides cover the issues that cost buyers the most money when they are missed — water supply, zoning, arena construction, and barn safety. Read them before you make an offer on any property.

Complete Buyer's Guide

The full overview — water, zoning, arena, barn, title, and why the agent you choose matters more on a residential property than on any other transaction. Start here if you are new to residential property buying or new to the Cave Creek market.

Wells & Water — What to Test Before You Buy

In the Arizona desert, water defines a residential property. This guide covers pump test requirements, GPM thresholds for different herd sizes, storage tank evaluation, and water quality testing — everything you need to evaluate a well before closing.

Zoning & Ag Exemptions

Cave Creek parcels fall either inside the Town of Cave Creek, with its Desert Rural ordinance, or in unincorporated Maricopa County under County zoning. This guide covers how to confirm jurisdiction, what Desert Rural means for horse-keeping (including the two-contiguous-acre threshold, setbacks, and lighting limits), and how the Maricopa County agricultural classification can reduce your property tax.

  • Zoning & Desert Rural Rules

Arena Inspection — Footing, Dimensions, Drainage

An arena is the most expensive component to replace on a residential property and the most commonly misrepresented in listings. This guide covers how to measure and evaluate footing depth, identify base problems, and assess drainage — the issues that a photograph will never reveal.

  • Arena Inspection — Footing, Dimensions, and Drainage

Barn Evaluation — Ventilation, Electrical, Stalls

Arizona barns fail differently than barns in humid climates. Heat and ventilation failure are the primary dangers — not rot or moisture. This guide covers the desert-specific barn evaluation checklist: ventilation gaps, covered runs, electrical safety, and stall dimensions.
